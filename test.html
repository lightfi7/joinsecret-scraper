      DigitalOcean is a cloud infrastructure provider offering a user-friendly platform for deploying and scaling applications. It allows users to create virtual machines (droplets) and run applications swiftly. The platform features a straightforward interface, robust API, and comprehensive documentation for efficient infrastructure management. DigitalOcean also provides various products and features like managed databases, load balancers, and Kubernetes to aid businesses in scaling and optimizing applications. Essentially, DigitalOcean enables developers to concentrate on building and deploying applications, eliminating the need to manage complex infrastructure.

      Pros from reviewers

      • Affordable and transparent pricing: Digital Ocean offers cost-effective solutions for small to medium businesses, with clear and upfront pricing that allows users to know exactly how much they will pay beforehand

      • Robust and reliable servers: Digital Ocean's servers are robust and dependable, providing a stable platform for hosting, databases, and backup services

      • Wide array of options and features: From easy deployment and server resizing to domain management and floating IPs, Digital Ocean offers a variety of options and features that cater to unique use cases

      • User-friendly interface: Digital Ocean's user interface is intuitive and easy to navigate, allowing users to carry out tasks in the browser without using CLI

      • Excellent customer service: Despite some negative experiences, many users have praised Digital Ocean's customer service for being helpful and responsive, resolving issues quickly and efficiently.

      Cons from reviewers

      • Data loss after upgrades: Some users have reported losing several days of data after upgrading their managed database

      • Payment issues: Some users have experienced problems with their prepaid visa cards being rejected by the system, and alternative payment methods resulted in additional fees

      • Limited management options: Despite its easy deployment and unique use cases, Digital Ocean offers fewer management options compared to other cloud service providers

      • Slow customer support: There have been complaints about slow response times from the support team, and in some cases, issues were not resolved

      • Inaccessibility due to networking issues: There have been instances where the networking was not working, making the platform inaccessible for some users.

      vs

      DigitalOcean and Azure are two service providers that offer different prices and a variety of features. What sets DigitalOcean apart is its reputation for pricing and transparent fee structure. Their popular droplet can be obtained at a rate of $0.015 per hour or $10 per month. On the other hand, Azure offers higher pricing due to the ongoing price competition among major cloud providers. Their A0 Linux instance comes at a cost of $0.02 per hour or approximately $15 per month.


      While Azure provides features and seamless integration with Microsoft products, DigitalOcean has made significant advancements in expanding its offerings. They now offer clustering, scalability, networking, and monitoring features alongside their IaaS environment for Linux servers. Moreover, they provide a one-click setup for integrated applications, a control panel with management functions SSD, only disks for enhanced performance, and a quick 55-second provisioning limit for instances.
